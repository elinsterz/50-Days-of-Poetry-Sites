Once, my very best darling, the sea and the land were all one mass and the light was confused and hadn’t found a place to rest. And, Megan, love, my sister’s eyes were not yet there to hold it all together since she hadn’t yet been born so when the world dropped out from under us and no one, not the on-calls with their CAT scans, not the sovereign souls who monitor the twilit room where newborns come to die or live, when no one could tell us if you would be one of the lucky ones able to walk and speak and only this, the one unstinted blessing fate had given us to give you was a sister in whose eyes you were the sun and moon, it meant we all no matter what befell us all had solid ground.    Pity the part we think we do on purpose.
