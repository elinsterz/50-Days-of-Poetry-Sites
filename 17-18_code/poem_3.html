3.

Second-born. As fateful as the transit to light and air or so you’ve often tried to teach me I will never properly understand. But I know how the hair at your temples curls in summer when the air is moist. As if she’d been returned to me. I must have had some under-the-radar notion even then when we were children how that little looseness threw my petty masteries in the shade. And so the joy of it was lost on me. Till you. I’m the only person living who remembers her childhood curls.

Love Poem by Linda Gregerson
